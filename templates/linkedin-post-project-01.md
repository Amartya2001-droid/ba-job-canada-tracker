# LinkedIn Post Draft: Project 1

I recently analyzed Canadian healthcare wait-time data using a public Statistics Canada dataset.

Question I wanted to answer:
Where do headline wait-time numbers hide more serious access pressure?

What I found:
- Non-emergency surgeries had the longest tail delay in the current output, with a 95th percentile wait time of 34.7 weeks.
- Specialist visits and selected diagnostic tests both reached 26.0 weeks at the 95th percentile.
- Median waits were much lower than tail waits, which shows why percentile-based reporting matters.

Tools used:
- SQLite
- SQL
- Power BI workflow planning

This project helped me think more like a Business Analyst by focusing on what a healthcare leader would actually need to know, not just what charts look interesting.
