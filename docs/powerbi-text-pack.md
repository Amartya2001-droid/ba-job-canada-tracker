### Subtitle

`Coverage is shown as facilities per 100,000 residents`

### KPI Labels

- `Highest Coverage`
- `Lowest Coverage`
- `Total Facilities Represented`

### Insight Card Title

`What This Means`

### Insight Card Body

`Prince Edward Island leads the current comparison at 52.01 facilities per 100,000 residents, while Alberta is lowest at 5.91. This shows why healthcare access should be evaluated with population-normalized metrics rather than raw facility totals alone.`

### Footer Data Note

`Data note: Ontario is included after normalizing the source label "Ontario by Ontario Health Region" to province-level Ontario.`

### Screenshot Caption Idea

`Current province-level output shows strong variation in normalized healthcare facility coverage, with Prince Edward Island highest and Alberta lowest in the present comparison.`
